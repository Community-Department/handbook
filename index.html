Section 01

Welcome

Welcome to the Community Department Handbook

This handbook serves as the official reference guide for all members of the Community Department within the Tedder Management Staff Team of Clockwork's United State of America (CUSA). It outlines the expectations, responsibilities, procedures, and policies that every member is expected to understand and uphold.

The Community Department is the backbone of CUSA's moderation and community engagement efforts. We are responsible for maintaining a safe, fair, and welcoming environment for all members of the community. This handbook has been crafted to ensure that every member of the team operates with consistency, professionalism, and dedication.

This document serves all ranks within the Community Department — from Community Moderators (CMOD) to the Community Director (CD). Whether you are newly appointed or a seasoned member, this handbook is your go-to resource for all department guidelines.

Classification Notice
This handbook is strictly classified for authorized Community Department personnel only. Do not share, reproduce, or distribute any part of this document outside the department.

Section 02

Introduction

Department Scope

The Community Department supports the Moderation Department with all non-military community interactions including Discord moderation, in-game support, ticket resolution, community events (CD only), giveaway administration (CD only), and rank logging for eligible personnel:

  • Moderating the CUSA game servers and Discord community to ensure a safe environment for all players.
  • Planning, organizing, and hosting community events and giveaways to engage the playerbase.
  • Enforcing the CUSA ruleset fairly and consistently across all platforms.
  • Documenting all moderation actions, event activity, and rank changes to maintain transparency.
  • Supporting and nurturing new community members and staff team recruits.
Core Values
These are the principles that guide every member of the Community Department in their day-to-day work.

Integrity

We uphold honesty and transparency in all department actions and decisions.

Professionalism

We conduct ourselves with respect, discipline, and maturity at all times.

Dedication

We are committed to the growth and well-being of the CUSA community.

Fairness

We enforce rules and policies without bias, favoritism, or prejudice.

Teamwork

We collaborate and support one another to achieve the department's goals.

Work Platforms
The Community Department operates across the following platforms. All members are expected to maintain an active and professional presence across these spaces.
  1. Roblox — Primary game platform where moderation and community events take place within CUSA.
  2. Discord — Main communication hub for all department meetings, announcements, logs, and coordination.
  3. Department Logs Channel — Where all official logs — moderation, event, and rank logs — are submitted by department members.
  4. Request Channel — Used for submitting lower quota, VC and other requirements inside quota with a valid reason.
  5. LoA Channel — Leave of Absence channel for members who need to temporarily step away from their duties.

Section 04

Events & Giveaways

Community events and giveaways are essential for maintaining player retention and fostering positive relations between CUSA Staff Team and community engagement.
Event Protocol
Event Information
The Community Department is authorized to host the following types of community events. View our Community Event Games Document for a full list of games we use.
Event Types
The Community Department is authorized to host the following types of community events:

Game Nights

Organized sessions where community members play Roblox games together, fostering team spirit and community bonding.

Trivia Events

Quiz-style events covering CUSA lore, general knowledge, or Roblox topics.

Tournaments

Competitive events where community members compete against each other in specific games or challenges.

Holiday Events

Special themed events organized around real-world or Roblox holidays and celebrations.

Obby

Games where people do parkour/obby challenges.

Horror Games

Horror games where people team together to complete a story or defeat something.

Event Logging

Proper logging is mandatory for all events hosted by Community Department members. Failure to document events may result in the event not counting toward the weekly quota.

Event Log Format

Logs must include the event name, event description, and host name. (Inside community event channel, not the event logs channel)

Giveaway Description
Giveaway Information

Giveaways are a great way to reward our community and boost engagement. All giveaways must follow the guidelines below to ensure fairness and compliance with CUSA policies:

  • Giveaways may be sponsored by yourself or another member of the Community Department. However, you require permission from CD to host one as they are able to coordinate with the giveaway department in CET.
Important Note for Moderators
Moderators are reminded that giveaway hosting is a privilege, not a right. All giveaway winners must be publicly announced in the designated channel. Private giveaways are prohibited and are not to be hosted by any of the Community Moderators.
Section 05

Weekly Quota

All active members of the Community Department are required to meet their weekly quota. Quotas reset every week and must be logged every Saturday in the designated logs channel. Failure to meet quota without an approved exemption may result in disciplinary action.
Quota Deadline
Quota is logged every Saturday. All logs must be submitted before end of deadline.
Admin-only: Quota values are editable by CD and CSEC ranks.

Quota Exemptions

Members who are unable to meet their weekly quota due to personal circumstances may request an exemption. Approved exemptions will prevent disciplinary action for the week in question. To request a quota exemption:

Option 1 — Request Channel

Submit a formal exemption request in the #request channel. Include your reason and the duration of your requested exemption.

Option 2 — Leave of Absence (LoA)

Post in the #loa channel if you require an extended absence. LoA automatically grants a quota exemption for the approved duration.

Warning
Failure to meet quota without an approved exemption will result in a formal strike, and continued non-compliance may lead to demotion or removal from the Community Department.
Section 06

Chain of Command

The Community Department operates under a structured chain of command. All members are expected to follow this hierarchy when reporting issues, escalating matters, or seeking guidance. Bypassing the chain of command without just cause is not permitted.
High Command

Community Director (CD) High Command

The highest authority within the Community Department. The Community Director sets the direction of the department and oversees all operations. They work directly alongside other High Command and Senior Management.

Middle Command

Community Secretary (CSEC) Middle Command

Middle command who directly assists the Community Director. Community Secretaries handle rank changes and supervise lower command.

Low Command

Senior Community Moderator (SCMOD) Low Command

Experienced moderators who have proven their dedication and skill within the Community Department. They serve as mentors to Community Moderators and assist them when needed.

Community Moderator (CMOD) Low Command

The foundation of the Community Department. Community Moderators are responsible for maintaining a welcoming atmosphere for all community members.

Chain of Command Guidelines
  1. Always address concerns or issues to the rank directly above you first before escalating further.
  2. Community Moderators should report to Senior Community Moderators for guidance.
  3. Senior Community Moderators should escalate significant issues to a Community Secretary.
  4. Community Secretaries bring matters requiring high-level decisions to the Community Director.
  5. In the absence of the Community Director, Community Secretaries are able to help and guide you.
  6. Emergency situations (e.g., severe rule violations, raids) may warrant direct escalation to the highest available rank.
