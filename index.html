Document Notice

This handbook serves as the official guide for all members of the Community Department within Clockwork's United States of America (CUSA). All personnel are expected to read, understand, and abide by the policies outlined herein. Failure to comply may result in disciplinary action.

Introduction

Purpose and scope of the Community Department

Purpose

The Community Department exists to foster a positive, engaging, and well-moderated environment within CUSA. Our mission is to ensure that all community members feel welcome, safe, and heard while maintaining the standards and integrity of the community.

Department Overview

The Community Department is responsible for overseeing community interactions, organizing events, managing giveaways, enforcing community guidelines, and serving as the bridge between the community and Senior Management.

Authority

All Community Department members operate under the authority of the Community Director and Senior Management. Decisions made by High Command are final and binding. Members are expected to carry out their duties with professionalism, impartiality, and dedication.

Responsibilities

Duties and expectations by rank

Community Director (CD)

  • Oversee all Community Department operations
  • Approve or deny event proposals and major decisions
  • Manage department staff and conduct promotions/demotions
  • Represent the department in Senior Management meetings
  • Ensure all quotas and standards are maintained
  • Handle escalated issues and appeals

Community Secretary (CSEC)

  • Assist the Community Director in daily operations
  • Supervise Senior Community Moderators and Community Moderators
  • Review and approve moderation logs and rank logs
  • Coordinate events and giveaways
  • Handle staff reports and internal disputes
  • Act as CD in their absence

Senior Community Moderator (SCMOD)

  • Moderate community channels and enforce rules
  • Assist in organizing events and giveaways
  • Train and mentor Community Moderators
  • Submit weekly moderation logs
  • Report issues to CSEC or CD
  • Maintain weekly quota requirements

Community Moderator (CMOD)

  • Monitor community channels for rule violations
  • Issue warnings and enforce community guidelines
  • Participate in events and giveaways
  • Submit weekly moderation logs
  • Assist community members with questions
  • Maintain weekly quota requirements

Events & Giveaways

Guidelines for community engagement activities

Community Events

Community events are organized to increase engagement, reward active members, and strengthen community bonds. All events must be approved by CSEC+ before announcement.

Event Planning Process:
1. Submit event proposal to CSEC or CD
2. Await approval and feedback
3. Coordinate with relevant departments
4. Announce event with proper formatting
5. Execute and document the event
6. Submit post-event report

Giveaways

Giveaways are a key tool for community retention and growth. All giveaways must follow the established guidelines to ensure fairness and transparency.

  • All giveaways require CSEC+ approval
  • Prizes must be confirmed available before announcement
  • Winner selection must be random and verifiable
  • Prize distribution must be documented
  • No staff may participate in giveaways they organize

Documentation

All events and giveaways must be properly documented including: date, time, participants, outcomes, and any issues encountered. Documentation should be submitted within 24 hours of event conclusion.

Important Notes

  • Quotas reset every Sunday at 11:59 PM EST
  • Failure to meet quota for 2 consecutive weeks may result in disciplinary action
  • Rank logs are restricted to CSEC+ only
  • All logs must be submitted in the designated channels
  • LOA requests must be submitted before the quota period begins

Chain of Command

Hierarchy and reporting structure

High Command

Community Director (CD)

The highest authority within the Community Department. All major decisions, promotions, and disciplinary actions require CD approval.

Middle Command

Community Secretary (CSEC)

Directly reports to the CD. Oversees day-to-day operations, supervises Low Command, and acts as CD in their absence.

Low Command

Senior Community Moderator (SCMOD) & Community Moderator (CMOD)

Frontline staff responsible for community moderation, event support, and quota fulfillment. Reports to CSEC.

Reporting Protocol

  • CMOD reports to SCMOD or CSEC
  • SCMOD reports directly to CSEC
  • CSEC reports directly to CD
  • Escalate issues up the chain — never skip ranks without cause
  • In emergencies, any member may contact CD directly
